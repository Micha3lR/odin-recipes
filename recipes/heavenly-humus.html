Heavenly Humus

Fill your soul!

This is a dish of divine inspiration, a culinary creation that transcends the ordinary. It’s a harmonious blend of flavors that evokes the sacred and the sublime, inviting those who partake to savor a taste of the celestial. Each spoonful is a silent hymn, a tribute to the stories and wonders that have shaped our spiritual heritage.

This humus is more than just a dish; it’s an experience, a moment where the earthly and the heavenly converge. It’s a reminder of the beauty and mystery that lie beyond our sight, a humble offering that connects us to the timeless tales of faith and creation.

Ingredients

Manna Chickpeas
  • 2 cups of chickpeas, nourished by the dew of heaven
Tahini from the Tree of Life
  • 1/3 cup plus 1 tablespoon of tahini, made from seeds of the sacred tree
Cumin of Canaan
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin, harvested from the spice gardens of the Promised Land
Garlic from the Garden of Eden
  • 3 cloves of garlic, plucked from the earth of paradise
Lemon Juice of the River Jordan
  • Juice of 1/2 a lemon, blessed by the waters where John was baptized
Olive Oil from Mount Olive
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil, pressed from the olives grown on the mount of peace
Salt from Lot's Wife
  • A pinch of salt, a reminder of the enduring covenant
Water from Bethesda's Pool
  • 1/2 to 1 cup of water, stirred by the angel's touch

Steps

  1. Soak the Chickpeas

    • Begin by soaking the manna chickpeas overnight in a bowl of water, ensuring they're fully submerged. The chickpeas will expand, so make sure there's enough water.

  2. Cook the Chickpeas

    • Drain the soaked chickpeas and transfer them to a pot. Cover them with fresh water and bring to a boil

    • Reduce the heat and simmer until they're tender, about 1.5 to 2 hours.

  3. Prepare the Tahini Mixture

    • In a separate bowl, combine the tahini from the Tree of Life with the lemon juice of the River Jordan. Whisk together until smooth and creamy.

  4. Blend the Ingredients

    • Once the chickpeas are cooked, drain them and add to a food processor.

    • Include the garlic from the Garden of Eden, cumin of Canaan, and salt from Lot's wife.

    • Blend until you get a smooth paste.

  5. Combine with Tahini Mixture

    • Add the tahini mixture to the food processor with the chickpea paste.

    • While blending, slowly pour in the olive oil from Mount Olive and water from Bethesda's Pool until you reach the desire consistency.

  6. Adjust the Flavors

    • Taste your humus and adjust the flavors as needed.

    • You might want to add more lemon juice for tanginess or salt for seasoning

  7. Serve with Blessings

    • Transfer the humus to a serving dish.

    • Create a well in the center and pour a little more olive oil.

    • Garnish with a sprinkle of cumin or sumac, and perhaps some parsley for a pop of green.
